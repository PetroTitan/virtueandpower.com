## The wisdom of action

Practical wisdom — Aristotle's *phronesis* — is the intellectual virtue of
knowing how to act well in particular situations, and the platform reads it as
one of his most important and most useful concepts. It is distinct from
theoretical wisdom (*sophia*), the knowledge of eternal truths: practical
wisdom concerns the changeable world of human action, where the question is not
what is always true but what is the right thing to do *here, now, for this
person, in these circumstances*. The platform reads it as the master-virtue of
[ethics](/themes/virtue-ethics) and politics — the capacity for good judgement
that no rule or formula can replace.

## Why rules are not enough

The platform reads Aristotle's deepest insight as the recognition that ethics
cannot be reduced to rules. Particular situations are infinitely various, and
no general principle can specify in advance how it applies to every case; the
person of practical wisdom *perceives* what a situation requires, weighing the
relevant features and finding the right action, often the mean between excess
and deficiency. The platform reads this under [virtue
ethics](/themes/virtue-ethics): the virtuous person is not one who follows a
rule-book but one who has the trained perception and judgement to see and do
what is fitting — a skill acquired through experience, not learned from a text.

## The knowledge of the statesman

The platform reads practical wisdom as the knowledge proper to the *statesman*.
Where Plato sought to ground politics in the theoretical knowledge of the Good,
Aristotle grounds it in practical wisdom — the experienced judgement that knows
how to legislate, deliberate and act well in the messy, particular,
ever-changing world of actual cities. The platform reads this as the heart of
the difference between the two: Aristotle's statesman is wise not because he has
grasped eternal Forms but because he has the practical judgement to find the
good achievable *in these circumstances*. It is the virtue of the leader, read
in [practical wisdom and leadership](/essays/practical-wisdom-and-leadership).

## Why it matters for Virtue & Power

Practical wisdom is the keystone of Aristotle's ethics and politics, and the
platform reads it as one of the most valuable ideas the classical tradition
offers to anyone who must actually act and lead. It connects his thought to the
platform's reading of [leadership and
character](/themes/leadership-and-character) and [statecraft](/themes/statecraft),
and it is the practical counterweight to the Platonic dream of governing by
theoretical knowledge alone.
